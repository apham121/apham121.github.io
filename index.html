Alex Pham

Mechanical Engineering

As a child, I grew up fascinated by anything with wheels, wings, or gears, playing pretend with any object in the house as if I drove a thousand horsepower car in my living room or was a pilot of a F-16 through the kitchen. My intense curiosity lead me to my career as a mechanical engineering student at University of California: Riverside. I am a current member of the Highlander Racing Team where I live out the start of a childhood dream building a small scale EV formula style car.

In Highlander Racing FSAE, I am apart of the Chassis and Driver Interfaces team. My primary focus in helping the team is rooted in design and manufacturing, using SOLIDWORKS to create and validate parts that will be fabricated in house by the team. For our 2024 car, I am solely resonsible for the design and fabrication of the steering wheel and pedal box assembly, both of which were fabricated in house by myself and other talented engineers on the team.

Outside of Highlander Racing, I partake in FPV (First Person View) Quadcopter building and flying, capturing breathtaking scenary in nature, or challengeing myself in daunting acrobatic "Freestyle" flying. I am also an avid car ethusiast who maintains, repairs, and modifies my 2013 Toyota GT86 into a reliable, sporty car that can carve canyons roads and attack corners at local Southern California tracks.
